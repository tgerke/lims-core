<h2 id="why-lims-core">Why lims-core</h2>

<div class="landing-grid">
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>Compliance as schema, not as feature</h3>
    <p>
      The audit trail is hash-chained and written by database triggers, results
      are versioned rows that can never be overwritten, and signatures are bound
      by hash to the exact record version they approve. The app connects as a
      least-privilege role that cannot alter any of it.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>Biobank and analytical on one core</h3>
    <p>
      Samples, tests, results, storage, and custody are modeled once. The
      biobank workflow came first; the analytical layer — specifications,
      worksheets, QC controls with Westgard rules, certificates of analysis — is
      built on the <em>same</em> core as modules, not a rewrite.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>A modern, hireable stack</h3>
    <p>
      TypeScript, PostgreSQL, and React, not a legacy Java/Oracle platform. That
      is a far easier maintenance and hiring story, and it is why the compliance
      controls could be pushed down into the database in the first place.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>Immutable by construction</h3>
    <p>
      Audit events, custody, results, and signatures reject <code>UPDATE</code>
      and <code>DELETE</code> by trigger. The runtime role cannot insert audit
      rows at all, so there is no path — not even a buggy one — to rewrite
      history.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>Open source, no lock-in</h3>
    <p>
      AGPL-3.0 licensed: nobody can take this code and sell it back to you as a
      closed platform. Your specimen data and its full custody record live in a
      Postgres database you own and can query directly.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="landing-card">
    <h3>Honest about scope</h3>
    <p>
      This is a broad, working build on a production-shaped compliance core, not
      a validated production system and not a drop-in replacement for a large
      commercial LIMS. The <a href="/lims-core/roadmap/">roadmap</a> is specific
      about where the line is.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
